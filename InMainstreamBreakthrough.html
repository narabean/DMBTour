Mainstream Breakthrough

DMB's second album, "Under the Table and Dreaming," was released in 1994 and featured hits like "What Would You Say" and "Ants Marching." This album marked their mainstream breakthrough. They continued to tour extensively, building a reputation for their improvisational live performances. In 1996, they released "Crash," which included the hit single "Crash into Me." "Before These Crowded Streets" (1998) showcased their musical versatility and experimentation. Tragedy struck in 1993 with the death of LeRoi Moore in an ATV accident. He was replaced by Jeff Coffin. The band continued to release successful albums and became known for their annual summer tours.
