Evolution and Transition

The band's sound evolved, incorporating elements of jazz, rock, and world music into their music. They released albums like "Everyday (2001), "Busted Stuff" (2002), and "Stand Up" (2005). Boyd Tinsley left the band in 2018, and they continued to tour and record with guest musicians. DMB released "Big Whiskey & the GrooGrux King" in 2009 as a tribute to LeRoi Moore, featuring his final recordings. The band continued to maintain a strong fan base and performed annual summer tours.
